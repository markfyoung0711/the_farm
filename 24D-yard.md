
To treat 2 acres of Bermuda grass with your 25-gallon NorthStar sprayer, here is the breakdown for the most effective professional-grade mix (Celsius + Certainty) and the most budget-friendly mix (2,4-D).
These calculations assume a standard calibration of 12.5 gallons per acre (meaning you will use one full 25-gal tank per acre, for a total of two tanks).
Option 1: The "Pro Mix" (Celsius + Certainty)
Best for killing almost everything (broadleaf, grassy weeds, and sedges) without harming Bermuda.

| Ingredient | Amount Per 25-gal Tank (1 Acre) | Total for 2 Acres |
|---|---|---|
| Celsius WG | 4.9 oz (by weight) | 9.8 oz |
| Certainty | 1.25 oz (small scoops) | 2.5 oz |
| Surfactant | 8 fl oz | 16 fl oz |
| Blue Marker Dye | 4–8 fl oz | 8–16 fl oz |

Option 2: The "Budget Mix" (2,4-D Amine)
Best for basic broadleaf weeds like dandelions, clover, and plantain.

| Ingredient | Amount Per 25-gal Tank (1 Acre) | Total for 2 Acres |
|---|---|---|
| 2,4-D Amine 4 | 3 pints (48 fl oz) | 6 pints (96 fl oz) |
| Surfactant | 8 fl oz | 16 fl oz |
| Blue Marker Dye | 4–8 fl oz | 8–16 fl oz |

Square Footage Reference
If you need to spot-treat or measure smaller sections, use these numbers:

* 1 Acre = 43,560 sq. ft.
* 2 Acres = 87,120 sq. ft.
* Your Tank (25 gal) = Covers 43,560 sq. ft.
* Application Rate: ~0.5 gallons of mixed solution per 1,000 sq. ft.

Quick Setup Checklist

   1. Fill the tank halfway with water first.
   2. Add your herbicide(s) and agitate (shake or bypass return).
   3. Add the surfactant and blue dye last (to prevent excessive foaming).
   4. Top off with the remaining water.
   5. Spray when the wind is low (under 10 mph) and no rain is expected for 24 hours.

Note: If your sprayer covers 1 acre in less than a full tank (e.g., you finish the acre and still have 5 gallons left), you are driving too fast. You must either slow down or increase the herbicide concentration per gallon to match your speed.
