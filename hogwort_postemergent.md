# Hogwort (Goatweed) - Post-Emergent Treatment

Post-emergent herbicides work on actively growing broadleaf weeds and brush. Apply at green-up for best results. Since Remedy is **not a pre-emergent**, plan on follow-up applications as new seedlings emerge.

---

## 25-Gallon North Star Sprayer — 1 Acre Mix (209 x 209 ft patch)

| Component | Amount |
|---|---|
| **Water** | 20 gallons |
| **2,4-D** | 1 quart (32 oz) |
| **Remedy (triclopyr)** | 8 oz |
| **Surfactant** | 8 oz |

---

## Mixing Order

1. Fill tank with **~10 gallons of water**
2. Add **2,4-D** with agitation running
3. Add **Remedy**
4. Add **surfactant**
5. Top off to 20 gallons
6. Keep agitating throughout application

## License Requirements

> **2,4-D requires a pesticide applicator license** in most states for commercial/agricultural use. Verify your state's requirements before purchasing or applying.

---

## General Notes

- This mix covers **1 acre (209 x 209 ft)** per tank
- If the mixture stands over **4 hours**, re-agitate before spraying
- Mix only what you'll use in a single day's work
- Remedy is **not a pre-emergent** — follow up as new seedlings emerge
