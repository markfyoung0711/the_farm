# Hogwort (Goatweed) - Post-Emergent Treatment

Post-emergent herbicides work on actively growing broadleaf weeds and brush. Apply at green-up for best results.

## Product: Remedy (triclopyr)

Remedy is effective at knocking back existing hogwort/goatweed plants. Since it is **not a pre-emergent**, plan on follow-up applications as new seedlings emerge.

---

## Mixing Instructions — 25-Gallon North Star Sprayer (Broadcast/Ground Spray)

**Rate:** 2 pints of Remedy per 10 gallons of water per acre

| Component | Amount for 25 Gallons |
|---|---|
| **Water** | ~24 gallons |
| **Remedy** | ~5 pints (80 oz) |
| **Surfactant (Dawn)** | ~2–3 oz |

---

## Mixing Order

1. Fill tank about **half full with water** (~12 gal)
2. Add **Remedy** and agitate
3. Add **surfactant**
4. Fill remainder with water to 25 gallons
5. Keep agitating throughout application

---

## Surfactant Notes

- Dawn dish soap works as a non-ionic surfactant in a pinch — use about **1 tsp per gallon** (~2–3 oz for 25 gallons)
- Agricultural surfactants like **Alligare 90** or **Hi-Yield Spreader Sticker** will outperform Dawn
- Add agricultural surfactant during final tank fill if using a water dilution spray (not an oil-water emulsion)

---

## General Notes

- If the mixture stands over **4 hours**, re-agitate before spraying
- Mix only what you'll use in a single day's work
